Note on condos & construction costs
Condo mortgage payments exclude HOA fees, which average ~$762/month statewide — among the highest in the nation. At $310–$520/sf for multifamily hard costs alone, up ~30% since 2020, building to price points accessible to median-income households requires substantial public subsidy. Affordability index: 100 = perfectly affordable at median income; below 100 = median income falls short.

Note on construction cost indices
The cost indices track the low end of each published RLB cost range (e.g., $310/sf for multifamily, not $520/sf), rebased to Q1 2020 = 100. Using the low end understates actual cost growth for higher-complexity projects, so the increases shown — +51% multifamily, +19% single-family, +81% ground parking — should be read as a floor, not a ceiling.

Methodology
Headline grocery figures are anchored to the USDA Thrifty Food Plan for Hawaiʻi — a federally calibrated minimum-cost food budget for the reference family of four (two adults 20–50, two children ages 6–8 and 9–11). County-level variation comes from our priced staple basket below (Honolulu = 100 index). Hawaiʻi's 4.5% General Excise Tax is applied at the register.

Our tracked basket covers staple SKUs collected from member/loyalty pricing at Foodland (Maikaʻi), Safeway (Club Card), Costco, Walmart, Target, and Don Quijote, then combined using per-county market-share weights derived from USDA SNAP retailer data. It is a price index, not a complete household food budget — useful for comparing counties month-to-month. The USDA TFP number reflects the full household food basket as calibrated by USDA.
